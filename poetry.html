When the Moon says
O child o child,
In this night and all that has come,
I shine the most and make all the stars like tiny dots;
The sky at night is all mine, without me it is dark and void.
I bring up the tides making all the oceans alive,
What do you know O child about my might?
The men look up at me, they pray me and fear me.
But you O child, you are of a different kind,
When I look at you every night sitting under my light
Goes away all my pride...
Cause however mighty i might be,
I shine the brightest only once in a while And fade away until I am none.
But you O child you toil and struggle,
You serve and care, you pick up the frail,
You hold down the pain;
And repeat these tasks day after day.
Yet here you are, like everyday sitting in your chair
And looking up at me and wondering,
If I am bored looking at you,
No my sweet child ...
You are an angel that shines brighter than me.

My Dear inquired of me, “What is peace?”!
You are standing in the meadows
In between the long green grass
Waiting for someone
Wearing a long white gown
You shine as bright as diamonds.
I come riding by the lake
Riding a horse as white as snow
I come gracefully towards you
But there is also a bit of haste in my movements
Cause I am eager to meet you.
I come and halt besides you
Mesmerized by your beauty
You smile back at me
And that my love..
is what I want in this whole wide world.
You offer me your hand
I bend and put my fingers in yours
Grab you by the waist
And pull you to my steed
Placing your tender body in front of me.
I seize the reigns, imprisoning you between my arms
Feeling your soft hair in my face
I put my chin on your shoulder
I pull the reigns
We move forward towards the woods
And I whisper in your ears-
“And this my love is what I call peace”.
